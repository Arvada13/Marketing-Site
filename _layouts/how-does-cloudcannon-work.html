1 Sync

Create a repository in GitHub or Bitbucket with your Jekyll or static site. You create and control the source code.

Connect your repository to CloudCannon and it will download and keep future updates in sync for you.

2 Live!

Your site is live on the web. CloudCannon optimises JavaScript, CSS and other assets so your site loads as fast as possible.

4 Build your own CMS components

Use Collections or Front Matter and YAML to build your own interface for updating complex content or metadata.

Build controls to manage any content on your site including photos in a gallery, the page title or hiding a page section.

5 Work your way

Continue working in your local environment with your favourite tools. To sync changes to CloudCannon, push to your Git repository.

Changes made in CloudCannon also get pushed back to the repository, so you always have an up-to-date version of the site.
