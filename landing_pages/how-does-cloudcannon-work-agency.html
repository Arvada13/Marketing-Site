Developers Clients

1 Sync

Create a repository in GitHub or Bitbucket with your Jekyll or static site. You create and control 100% of the source code.

Connect your repository to CloudCannon and it will download and keep future updates in sync for you.

2 Live!

Your site is live on the web. CloudCannon optimizes Javascript, CSS and other assets so your site loads insanely fast.

3 Share

Share your CloudCannon site with the Developers and Clients on your team.

Clients get a beautiful interface for updating Blogs, Collections and Front Matter. To make content on a page editable for Clients add class="editable" to elements in the HTML.

4 Build your own CMS components

Using Collections or Front Matter you can use YAML to build your own interface for updating complex content or metadata.

You can build controls to manage any content on your site including photos in a gallery, the page title or even hiding a page section.

5 Work your way

You can continue working in your local environment with your favourite tools. To sync changes to CloudCannon just push to your repository.

Changes made in CloudCannon also get pushed back to the repository so you can always pull in an up-to-date version of the site.

1 Preview

Once you share the site with your client, they can login and preview their site.

2 Update Content

Yellow boxes indicate the content they can update. If they click on one and update content directly on the page. What they see is exactly what it will look like on the live site.

3 Manage the Blog

If their site has a blog or collections they can manage all posts easily and visually. No coding required.

4 Complex Content

You can even manage complex content like photo galleries, products or the page title using a simple interface.

5 Live

Saved changes are instantly pushed to the live website. They can control over all the content on their site.
