How does CloudCannon work?

Developers Non-Developers

1 Sync

Create a repository in GitHub or Bitbucket with your Jekyll or static site. You create and control 100% of the source code.

Connect your repository to CloudCannon and it will download and keep future updates in sync for you.

2 Live!

Your site is live on the web. CloudCannon optimizes Javascript, CSS and other assets so your site loads insanely fast.

{% include customer-slideshow.html %}

3 Share

Share your CloudCannon site with your team.

Non-Developers get a beautiful interface for updating Blogs, Collections and Front Matter. To make content on a page editable for non-developers add class="editable" to elements in the HTML.

{% include proportional-youtube.html width=16 height=10 factor=70 youtube_id="FOYDMD1jA-0" %}

4 Build your own CMS components

Using Collections and Front Matter you can use YAML to build your own interface for updating complex content or metadata.

You can build controls to manage any content on your site including photos in a gallery, the page title or even hiding a page section.

5 Work your way

You can continue working in your local environment with your favourite tools. To sync changes to CloudCannon just push to your repository.

Changes made in CloudCannon also get pushed back to the repository so you can always pull in an up-to-date version of the site.

1 Preview

Once your Developer shares the site with you, login to CloudCannon get a live preview of the site.

2 Update Content

Yellow boxes indicate the content you can update. Click on one and update content directly on the page. What you see is exactly what it will look like on the live site.

3 Manage the Blog

If your site has a blog or collections you can manage all posts easily and visually. No coding or knowledge of markdown required.

4 Complex Content

You can even manage complex content like photo galleries, products or the page title using a simple interface.

5 Live

Saved changes are instantly pushed to the live website. Have control over all the content on your site.
